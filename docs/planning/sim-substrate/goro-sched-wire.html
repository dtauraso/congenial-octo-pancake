Goroutine + Scheduler (wire) — motion view

A goroutine's life is a path. The wire goes runQ → CPU → exit (default) or runQ → CPU → blocked-rail → back to runQ (parked / unparked). The CPU is one station; only one token sits in it at a time. Once a token enters a segment it rides end-to-end without pausing — the act of dispatching, of running through the quantum, and of being parked are all motion, not snapshot.

What is intentionally not shown: the buffer state of channels, the contents of the runQ as a static list, or the reason-for-blocking detail. Those are snapshot concerns and live on the other tab. Here the question is "what is the scheduler doing right now?"
