Chan (wire) — clean wire-diagram view

The wire is two things at once: the static direction of data flow, and the act of an item being delivered. Once a token is on the wire it rides end-to-end without pausing. Buffer state is not shown here — that's the other tab.

This is one item being delivered from A to B. From the wire's POV, channel capacity collapses to a boolean: is there ≥1 slot free at B? If yes, the item rides across; if no, the sender is blocked and nothing enters the wire. The actual count of free slots is irrelevant to the delivery act — it belongs to the snapshot view. Unbuffered channels are the cap=0 edge case where the predicate becomes "B has a receiver waiting right now." This predicate will be wired into the simulation in some form later.
